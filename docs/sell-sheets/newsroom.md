# For newsrooms & data-journalism desks

**The only unified, provenance-first API for Palestine — verified history plus
live West Bank ground truth, citable down to the record.**

## The problem you have

Covering Palestine means stitching together ACLED, OCHA, PCBS, B'Tselem,
Telegram, and a dozen PDFs — by hand, on deadline. Every figure needs a source
your editor and your lawyer will accept. Live events (a raid, a checkpoint
closure, a strike) break on Arabic Telegram hours before they reach a wire you
can cite.

## What you get

- **One API, 22 categories, 1948 → present.** Casualties, demolitions,
  detentions, displacement, settlements, health, education, water, economy —
  unified schema, stable ids, deduplicated.
- **Every number is citable.** Per-record permalinks, pinned daily snapshots
  (`?as_of=`), and per-source licensing so you know exactly what you can publish.
- **Live West Bank feed.** Structured alerts (raids, settler attacks, closures,
  strikes) classified from Palestinian channels in real time, geolocated, with
  the original source message id. ~245 live checkpoints with status + freshness.
- **Published accuracy.** `/quality/accuracy` gives you the precision/recall
  numbers per event type — defensible sourcing, not a black box.
- **Embeddable live tracker** for your site (white-label the map at
  `live.zaidlab.xyz`).

## Why it's different

Provenance-first: nothing is served without its source, timestamp, and trust
score. We publish our error rates. Built and operated from Ramallah — this is
ground truth, not a scrape.
